“Bespoke” Origin and Definition

The word "bespoke" itself is derived from the verb: "to bespeak," to "stand for something" or, in a specialized meaning: "to give order for it to be made" as to better reflect you. Often, "bespoke" can often be identified as a British term for tailored clothing like a made-to-measure shirt or suit; custom configured software could also be recognized as "bespoke." Early and mid-twentieth century tailors, shoemakers and automobile manufacturers were producing both "standard" and a highly personalized "bespoke" product line for those clients who sought even more individuality.

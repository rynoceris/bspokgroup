Direct to Garment Printing

home > services > direct to garment

Direct to Garment [DTG] printing is a rather new, state of the art technology, new to the screen printing industry within the past ten years. Direct to garment printing is the process of printing directly onto apparel using specialized inkjet printers engineered specifically for applications on textiles. So you ask, “Why direct to garment printing over screen printing?” Direct to Garment printing is really best for lower quantities [usually below 12 pieces] with multiple color images. Full color graphic images with unlimited colors are also perfect for the DTG printer.

's new Anajet MP-10 is capable of producing a precision image which rivals a color photograph on as few as one piece after quality artwork is prepared in under 2 minutes without all the costly 4 color process set-up fee’s typical of most shops without one! With screen printing you are limited on the amount of colors you can have per design, even though some of the largest industrial shops may have the newest fourteen color presses. If you have a very detailed logo you need printed, or low quantities, direct to garment [DTG] may be the way to go!

DTG print features:

  • Super soft, no feel on white or light garments without underbase.
  • Unlimited colors in design
  • NO setup fees with properly prepared Vector Art
  • Up to 12in. x 15in. print area
  • Prints on 100% cotton garments
  • Prints on light or dark garments [extra charge]
  • 6 piece minimum

Here are some helpful tips to get you started:

To get the very best quality digital print quality artwork is one of the most important factors when considering Direct to Garment printing. welcomes artwork that is complex, full of colors, gradients, or simply short runs that contain several different colors. If you only need a small run of shirts printed then it will likely be far less expensive to have them digitally printed.

In order to avoid additional art charges, or time delays, properly prepare the art file for submission. Also, this will enable us to get the best possible print quality with whatever files we are provided.

For our Anajet, the best possible prints come from artwork prepared for final output in the following manner:

  • CMYK PSD File 300 dpi.
  • Along with a transparent background, all layers should be merged together as one.
  • Artwork MUST also be sized to print before it is submitted, with all colors adjusted as you see fit.

All of these requirements must be fulfilled in order for us to quickly and effectively process your artwork. Remember - this is the most vital step in ensuring that your final product is as vibrant and stunning as it can possibly be. If artwork is not to size, there is no transparent background, or any other requirements are not met, we will either return your file to you or we will have our graphic design department process your artwork, which will both delay your order and add additional charges.

If you are familiar with Adobe Photoshop, when considering digital photography also suggests use of the BRIGHTNESS / CONTRAST sliders to add greater contrast to your images. This will help to bring out the brightest possible colors in your prints.

Other good tools are the FOREGROUND FILL (to replace duller areas of color with brighter shades of the same color), CURVES, COLOR BALANCE, and HUE / SATURATION. The goal is to make the image a little brighter than you actually want it to print (without washing out the color and detail, and ensuring you are in CMYK mode when you do it). This will ensure that the shirt comes out to your satisfaction the first time we print it.

What is Embroidery?

Embroidery is the art of decorating fabric or other materials with needle and thread or yarn. Modern day “machine” embroidery mimics old world hand embroidery, especially in the use of chain stitches; but, the “satin stitch” and hemming stitches of machine work rely on the use of multiple threads resembling hand work in their appearance, not their construction.

What are advantages of embroidery?

Embroidered designs are a great way to reveal creativity and add that professional “look” to your uniform, apron, hat, polo shirt or jacket. At times, large embroidery orders with multiple positions and high stitch count’s can have a longer lead time than other types of apparel customization like screen-printing or [DTG] printing. The wait is worth it…When embroidered apparel is given away or worn for self-promotion it yields positive first impressions, better marketing results and a higher perceived value!

Do you run embroidery items one at a time?

We usually only run one at a time for sew out swatch sample for approval, smaller orders, or personalization on hat backs, sleeves or blankets. Instead, on a daily basis four multi-head machines consisting of a pair of eight and six head machines are scheduled daily by the embroidery supervisor. This gives us flexibility to run the same design in multiples of six pieces and eight pieces at a time. Machines assignments take into account variables like due dates, thread colors, stitch counts, estimated running time and the overall design in general. A recent order for 3,000+ shirts with a logo on the sleeve and left chest occupied all twenty-eight heads in order to meet the established due date.

How does the embroidery machine know what to do?

The embroidery process starts with good art and clearly defined color assignments for each element of the design including the lettering. This artwork must first be “digitized” using specialized computer software. This digitizing process creates the standardized language the machine understands, controlling things like sequence, changing needles and thread colors, trimming; or the stitching speed on the item.

How is pricing set for shirts, hats, jackets and hoodies?

If it helps you plan or set budgets: The size of the design, number of stitches required to reproduce the design, quantity of units ordered, design placement, in addition to the item of choice for embroidery all impact the final delivered price to you. Typically, traditional machine embroidery is used on polo’s, jackets, woven items, caps, tote bags and much more; tackle twill embroidery is typically seen on crewneck’s, hoodies, thick t-shirts and jersey’s.

What is, “tackle-twill” or applique embroidery?

Tackle Twill [some call it applique] is the process of using precision cut pieces of wool, felt and typically nylon twill fabric carefully positioned and sewn in place of actual stitching. Felt letters give you an old-school collegiate look. Or, when you want to save stitches this approach with nylon twill fabric is probably the way to go. We have also become experts at Greek Letter Tackle Twill. In the last twenty-three years our sister company Campus Classics has shipped ten’s of thousands tackle twill hoodies. Not only is tackle-twill a great look; but, a perfect way to blend added style. We stock fun colors and can help source fabric’s that may also better reveal your group’s personality.

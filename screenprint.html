Screen Printing

home > services > screen printing

Screen Printing is a 2000 year old process that uses a woven mesh which allows a set amount of ink to pass through a blocking stencil onto the garment. The stencil attached to the woven mesh forms open areas of the mesh that allows the transfer of ink onto the garment. A squeegee is carefully moved across the screen stencil pushing ink through the woven mesh onto the open areas creating a screen print in said color that helps formulate the design. The number of colors in a screen print can range from one all the way up to fourteen [14]. Another popular form of screen printing is a 4 color process.

Screen printing is most widely used as a form of apparel decoration for small business, not-for-profit organizations, Fortune 500 companies, schools and churches. Special events, family reunions and spirit wear also consume screen printing. Like embroidery, screen-printing can also be very cost effective for promotions based upon factors like the number of ink colors, quantity, lead-time and garment choice.

SCREEN PRINTING (PLASTISOL)

Screen printing with plastisol ink is the most durable of all prints and it also allows for the most accurate reproduction of PMS colors because of opacity of the ink. Screens are used to transfer one color of ink at a time onto the garment. Therefore, spot color works best. To download or view a PDF displaying PMS colors, please click here.

DISCHARGE PRINTING

Water based inks work well on light garments, but not on dark garments. Discharge printing uses water based pigments with special discharge agents that will bleach out the color of the fabric and leave the desired ink color. No white mask is needed on dark garments and the print will feel like its part of the fabric.

OVERSIZED PRINTS

The all over print is gaining in popularity. It is achieved by printing on large scale screens. We can print up to 4 colors using 17" x 20" screens.

SCREEN PRINTING (WATER BASED)

Many people enjoy t-shirts with printing that has a vintage or soft hand feel. This is achieved through water based inks which do not have the vinyl feel of the plastisol inks. However, they are not as durable and are more difficult to work with. In addition, the transparent nature of the water based inks limit their usefulness on dark garments.

BURNOUT PRINTING

Burnout Printing is an advanced printable chemical that will dissolve natural fibers when heat is applied. We can do washes or all over prints with it on 50/50 fabric that give the desired worn out texture.
